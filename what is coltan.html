Shadows of Coltan: The Price of Technology

Between technology, conflicts and human rights

  • What is Coltan?

Coltan is a combination of two minerals: columbite and tantalite, with tantalite being the most valuable due to its high content of tantalum.
Tantalum is a rare metal known for its exceptional properties, including high resistance to corrosion, excellent conductivity, and the ability to withstand high temperatures.
These qualities make it indispensable in the production of electronic components, particularly in the form of capacitors used in devices like smartphones, video cameras, and gaming consoles.
The use of tantalum in modern electronics helps improve energy efficiency, as it allows for the miniaturization of components and better heat management, leading to significant energy savings.
The Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) holds around 80% of the world's coltan reserves, making it a crucial source for the global electronics industry. However, the extraction of coltan in the region is highly
controversial due to the connection between mining activities and ongoing conflicts, as well as human rights abuses. Despite this, coltan remains essential for powering next-generation technologies, offering improvements
in both performance and energy efficiency across a wide range of high-tech gadgets.
