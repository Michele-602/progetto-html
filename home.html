Coltan is a key component of our modern electronics, but its extraction in the Congo comes at a high human and environmental cost.

What is Coltan?

Coltan is a mixture of columbite and tantalite, used in capacitors in electronic devices.

Coltan in Democratic Republic of Congo

Democratic Republic of Congo has one of the world's largest reserves. Its extraction is often linked to armed conflicts and exploitation.

Environmental and social impact

Artisanal mining causes deforestation, child labor, and violence in the mining regions.

Solutions and alternatives

There are initiatives for the traceability of minerals and the recycling of electronic waste.
